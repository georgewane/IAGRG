      <p>
        The Gender in Physics Working Group of the Indian Physics Association
        organized <em>Pressing for Progress</em>, a national conference on
        gender equity in physics in India in 2019 at the University of Hyderabad.
      </p>

      <p>
        The meeting brought together participants across institutions and
        career stages to discuss structural barriers, representation,
        workplace culture, mentoring, and pathways toward meaningful reform.
      </p>

      <p>
        Based on the discussions and recommendations emerging from the
        conference, the <strong>Hyderabad Charter for Gender Equity in Physics</strong>
        was formalized and circulated for endorsements across the scientific
        community.
      </p>

      <h2>Objectives of the Charter</h2>

      <ul>
        <li>Promote equitable opportunities in hiring, mentoring and leadership.</li>
        <li>Strengthen institutional accountability and transparent processes.</li>
        <li>Encourage inclusive academic culture and safe workplaces.</li>
        <li>Improve representation across all career stages.</li>
        <li>Build long-term structural support systems.</li>
      </ul>

      <h2>Get Involved</h2>

      <p>
        Members of the community are encouraged to read the charter and
        support its goals through endorsement and discussion within their
        institutions.
      </p>
